## S03E26: Fairytale

### Game 1:Jinx

**Episode:** S03E26 - Fairytale

**How to Play:**
1. Two people must say the exact same word at the exact same time
2. As soon as this happens, one person quickly yells "Jinx!"
3. The person who yelled "Jinx" starts counting from 1 to 10
4. They have 10 seconds to tag/touch the other person
5. If they touch them within 10 seconds, nothing happens - game ends
6. If they don't touch them by the count of 10, they yell "Skint!"
7. The person who got "skinted" cannot speak anymore
8. The only way to break the curse is if someone says their name
9. The cursed person cannot say their own name or speak at all
10. They must wait until someone else says their name to talk again

**Materials Needed:**
- No materials required
- Just two or more people
- Space to move around for tagging

**How to Introduce It:**
Start by explaining the rules clearly before playing. Practice saying words together to understand what "at the exact same time" means. Make it clear that it's a fun game about quick reflexes and paying attention. You can introduce it naturally when siblings or family members accidentally say the same thing at the same time. Keep the "curse" lighthearted and make sure everyone knows someone will eventually say their name.

**Key Phrases (from the episode):**
- "Okay, in the eighties, if two people said the exact same word at the exact same time, like... Chook!"
- "...one of you would yell out 'jinx'!"
- "...which meant you had ten seconds to touch them."
- "Skint!"
- "And then you could never speak again."
- "The only thing that could break the curse was if someone said your name."

**Variations:**
1. **Reverse Jinx** - The person who says "Jinx" second has to chase the first person
2. **Team Jinx** - Play in teams where team members can help break each other's curses
3. **Jinx Categories** - Only certain word categories count (animals, colors, foods)
4. **Silent Jinx** - Instead of saying "Jinx" out loud, you have to point at the person
5. **Musical Jinx** - Play during car rides and only song lyrics count
6. **Time Extension** - Increase the countdown to 20 or 30 seconds for younger kids
7. **Safe Words** - Designate certain words that automatically break all curses
8. **Gesture Jinx** - The cursed person can use gestures to get someone to say their name
9. **Family Jinx** - Multiple people can be jinxed at once, making it chaos
10. **Kindness Jinx** - Someone can choose to say the cursed person's name immediately to be kind

## S03E27: Musical Statues

### Game 1: Musical Statues (Classic)

**Episode:** S03E27 - Musical Statues

**How to Play:**
1. Choose one person to be the DJ who controls the music
2. Everyone else stands in an open space ready to dance
3. When the music starts playing, everyone dances freely
4. The DJ stops the music suddenly at random times
5. When the music stops, everyone must freeze like a statue immediately
6. The DJ checks if anyone is still moving (including blinking, wobbling, or adjusting)
7. Anyone who moves is "out" and sits down
8. The music starts again and the game continues
9. The last person still "in" is the winner
10. The winner becomes the next DJ

**Materials Needed:**
- Music player (phone, tablet, speaker, or just singing)
- Upbeat, fun music that makes people want to dance
- Open space for dancing safely
- Optional: Let children take turns choosing songs

**How to Introduce It:**
Start by demonstrating what a "statue" looks like - completely frozen in place. Practice freezing and checking if anyone wobbles. Begin with shorter music intervals so younger children can succeed. Make the first round non-competitive so everyone understands the rules. Emphasize that the fun is in the dancing, not just winning.

**Key Phrases (from the episode):**
- "We're playing musical statues."
- "And... stop."
- "You blinked, you blinked, you blinked. You're all out."
- "Come on, do it properly! Or don't do it at all."
- "Get that body moving!"
- "You kept raising the roof!"

**Variations:**
1. **Animal Statues** - Freeze as different animals when music stops
2. **Silly Poses** - Must freeze in the silliest position possible
3. **Emotion Statues** - DJ calls out an emotion to freeze in (happy, sad, surprised)
4. **Partner Statues** - Dance and freeze with a partner
5. **Slow Motion** - When music stops, move in extreme slow motion instead of freezing
6. **Balance Challenge** - Must freeze on one foot
7. **Character Statues** - Freeze as your favorite character or superhero
8. **Musical Shapes** - Freeze making a specific shape (circle, star, letter)
9. **Copy Cat Statues** - Everyone must copy the DJ's frozen pose
10. **Theme Music** - Use music from movies and freeze as characters from that movie

### Rock, Paper, Scissors

**How to Play:**

### Bug Walls (Bug Protection)

**Episode:** S03E46 - Slide

**How to Play:**
1. When bugs keep crossing your slide or play area, brainstorm solutions together
2. Build small walls or barriers using natural materials
3. Try different designs: stick walls, leaf walls, sand barriers
4. Test if bugs can climb over, fly over, or go around
5. Problem-solve together when walls don't work
6. Discuss what bugs can do (walk, fly, climb)
7. Keep trying new solutions until you find what works
8. Always treat bugs gently in the process

**Materials Needed:**
- Natural building materials: sticks, leaves, stones, sand
- The area you're trying to protect (slide, sandbox, picnic spot)
- Observation skills
- Patience and problem-solving attitude

**How to Introduce It:**
This game emerged from Bluey and Lila's problem: bugs kept getting on their slide. Instead of giving up, Lila suggested making walls. When that didn't work for flying bugs, they tried other solutions. This teaches engineering thinking, problem-solving, and adapting when your first idea doesn't work.

- "What are we gonna do?"
- "Ooh, I know. If we make walls, the little bugs won't be able to get over them."
- "Oh, yeah!"
- "Lucky you're so smart, Lila."
- "Grasshoppers can fly?"
- "The walls aren't working. The bugs just fly over them."
